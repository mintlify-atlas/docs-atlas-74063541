PA_PVP is a copy-paste decision loop you load into any LLM. Give it a plan, artifact, trade-off, or uncertain situation — it returns a forced verdict (`DO NOW`, `DO LATER`, or `DISCARD`), one executable `[NEXT]` action, and a machine snapshot you can paste back to continue exactly where you left off.

## What PA_PVP does

PA_PVP converts vague situations into operational decisions. You give it something to decide — a plan, a draft artifact, a trade-off, or a stalled project — and it applies a deterministic behavioral contract to produce:

- **A forced verdict** — `DO NOW`, `DO LATER`, or `DISCARD`. No "it depends."
- **One `[NEXT]` action** — a single, observable, timebox-bounded step.
- **A machine snapshot** — a code block you paste back on the next run to continue delta-only.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Load the kernel">
    Copy the contents of `PA_PVP_full_v9.9.0_canonical.txt` into your LLM system prompt or paste it at the start of a conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe your situation">
    Ask in plain language. Wrap your item in a `<<<I ...>>>` block or just write naturally — the kernel parses both.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive your verdict">
    PA_PVP returns a single machine code block with `STATE: DECISION.TEST.FINAL`, your verdict queue, and one executable `[NEXT]` step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste and continue">
    Execute or simulate `[NEXT]`, then paste the entire previous output back. PA_PVP picks up from the prior state without any manual editing.
  </Step>
</Steps>
